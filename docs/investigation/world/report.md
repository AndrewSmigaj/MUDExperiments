# How the rooms are used — the play report

> **Status: SCRATCHPAD (investigation) — 2026-07-15 overnight design run.** Companion to
> [`map.md`](map.md) and [`rooms.md`](rooms.md). This is the report Andrew asked for: how
> every room earns its place in an actual session. The quality assessment and the three
> polish passes are appended at the end (§8+) as they happen, so the process is auditable.

## 1. The session, told as rooms

A session is ~5 hours of daylight plus the storm night, on the real-time clock. Here is the
shape of a competent-but-first-time party's day, told as movement through the map:

**Hour 1 — the wreck teaches.** The crash site plays as already built: dig through the
plane, split kit, frisk the pilot, learn the verbs. The first outdoor steps are short
radials: the treeline for tinder, the muskeg edge, the debris trail. The world's outer
rooms are all VISIBLE as invitations (lake-glare west from the fuselage top, the gouge
pointing northeast, running water audible faintly south on a still moment) but nothing
forces the door. The chart and the manual seed three plans at once — cabin, elevation,
signals — and the party can't fund all three. That argument is the game.

**Hour 2 — the first radial expeditions.** Whichever plan wins, its first leg crosses
teaching country: the muskeg's drifted channel punishes straight-line thinking on the way
to the lake; the strike path pays salvage immediately (boughs, the tire, the wing's drip)
on the way up; the north wood hands over squaw wood and the camp site on the way anywhere.
The tuition zones (drifted channel, inlet mouth, overflow bend) all sit on FIRST legs by
design — the map spends its cheap lessons early so the expensive country later is met with
skills, not luck.

**Hour 3 — commitment.** The stay-or-go fork stops being theoretical: the cabin round
trip (3h first time) fits the remaining light exactly once, the search-plane beat is
coming "sometime after midday" (the manual's SAR pages say overflights follow drainages —
the lake line), and the storm's first band is visibly filling the western sky from the
knob, if anyone climbed. Three coherent parties emerge naturally: **signal camp** (lake +
gear gouge + north wood fuel ferries), **expedition** (creek → trapline → homestead), or
**split co-op** (the design's favorite: signalers hold the lake while runners make the
cabin — say/shout/whisper across zones and the speech bands finally spend their budget).

**Hour 4–5 — execution under weather.** Heavy snow re-prices everything (map.md §5): the
lake crossing closes, the tunnel stays open, broken trails start refilling. Whatever the
party built by now — fire on the ice, wire snares working the runs, a sled under repair
in the woodshed — either pays or teaches. The near-miss beat lands here if their smoke
wasn't ready: the plane's engine-drone crossing the overcast, swelling, fading, gone (the
GDD's mandated gut-punch, staged over the lake rooms).

**Night.** The world is three lit rooms (map.md §5): the stove, a fire, or the fuselage
huddle. The night phase plays IN whichever room the day's decisions bought, and its
quality (warm cabin with lamplight and beans vs. a smoky bough-shelter vs. the shivering
hull) is the day's scorecard. The tree-well hollow and the warmth-floor rule guarantee the
worst night is survivable if played straight — misery, not memory-wipe.

## 2. The four rescue routes, as room itineraries

GDD §37–39's additive-confidence model, with each route's rooms and its DISTINCT scarce
resource (the §37 improvement made geographic):

| Route | Rooms | Scarce resource | Confidence events |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Stay-and-signal** | crash site, ice_flat, gear_gouge, north-wood fuel zones | *fuel logistics + wind engineering* | fire visible on ice; TIRE smoke column (black, man-made); maintained through the plane beat |
| **Beacon (ELT §38)** | tail_section (the ELT), dooryard (cable) or avionics (wire), fuselage_top or the_knob | *conductor + elevation* | antenna rigged; elevation multiplier; the 121.5 manual clue closes the loop |
| **Radio (§38 deep puzzle)** | cockpit (the set), the wreck's batteries, the_knob (line of sight) | *carry logistics + weather windows* | static → voices on the knob; a working exchange during a clear band |
| **Travel/shelter** | creek run, trapline, homestead | *navigation skill + daylight* | the stove lit (survival secured ≠ rescue, but it BUYS the second weather window and the etiquette note hints Galena is reachable in spring terms — rescue confidence via "we can outlast this") |

Every route crosses the survival economy (all fires eat the same wood; all trips eat the
same daylight) but no two routes compete for their KEY resource — wire, elevation, wind
engineering, and navigation are separate accounts, per the GDD's explicit improvement.

## 3. The economies, as room networks

- **Fuel**: squaw wood (starter, north wood) → dwarf birch/krumm/black-spruce twigs
  (kindling, everywhere priced in armloads) → deadfall + logjam (bulk, tool-priced) →
  drift log + far burn + woodshed (jackpots, distance/tool-priced). The gradient is
  deliberate: fire is always POSSIBLE, scale is always EARNED.
- **Water**: melt (anywhere + fuel tax) → blue ice (efficiency, lake) → riffle + water
  hole (free liquid, positioned at map edges so "free" costs travel).
- **Food**: rations (finite, crash) → cranberries/rose hips (trickle) → grouse/ptarmigan
  (skill shots) → snare lines (planning + wire) → the fishery (the scaling source, longest
  chain) → cabin stores (the depot, farthest). Calories scale with commitment, never with
  luck.
- **Warmth/clothing**: crash clothing → the pilot's jacket → seat covers/blankets (DR-25
  already) → marten fur (craft) → the loft trunk (jackpot). Plus the terrain layer:
  exposure bands make WHERE you work a clothing decision.
- **Mobility**: bare boots → broken trails (self-made capital) → game trails/causeway/
  tunnel (the world's own roads, knowledge-priced) → snowshoes (cache) → the repaired
  sled (the logistics endgame that makes the woodshed matter at the wreck).
- **Information**: the chart, the manual pages, the pilot's lucid line, the knob's view,
  the blaze protocol, ice-reading, track-reading. Information is the only economy with no
  mass, which is why the knob — pure information — justifies the map's hardest climb.

## 4. What each Scene contributes (the one-line audit)

- **Muskeg** — the snow-travel tutorial + tinder/forage trickle + the willow gate.
- **Lake** — the visual stage, the wind bill, the ice curriculum, the greed test (burn).
- **North wood** — the survival economy's heart + the forward camp + the mercy bivvy.
- **Strike path** — salvage arc + the orientation compass + the avgas set-piece.
- **Ridge** — the reveal, the weather oracle, elevation for §38, the one lethal opt-in.
- **Birch stand** — fire-craft mastery arc (bark → punk → chaga) + the antler + moose sign.
- **Creek** — the corridor, liquid water, the fishery, overflow (the south's toll gate).
- **Beaver pond** — reusable infrastructure, the anti-loot lesson, wire salvage, trailhead.
- **Trapline** — the storm-safe route + the navigation skill-check + Holt's characterization.
- **Homestead** — the endgame shelter, the tool/mobility jackpots, the sled puzzle, the
  emotional landing ("leave the box full").

No Scene exists for scenery alone; each carries at least one economy node, one skill, and
one story beat. (The assessment below stress-tests this claim.)

## 5. Co-op, as geography

The map is sized so that two-to-four players are RICHER than one, never merely faster:
the cache's shoulder-stand and the sled's ferry legs literally prefer two bodies; split
parties keep contact along designed sight/sound seams (lake ↔ shore, knob ↔ valley
[shout + the §15 bands], the tunnel's blaze-relay in a whiteout); and the signal/expedition
split (§1) gives each half of a party its own complete gameplay day that RECOMBINES at
night (the sled arriving at the lake fire with cabin wood is the co-op fantasy image). The
propagator's distant-figure rendering finally has distances worth rendering.

## 6. How the world teaches (the knowledge ladder)

The map's skills each have a tutorial room, a paying room, and an exam room:

| Skill | Taught (cheap) | Pays | Examined (expensive) |
|---|---|---|---|
| snow travel | drifted_channel | everywhere | storm-phase travel |
| ice reading | inlet_mouth (3 telegraphs) | blue ice, crossings | confluence hole-chop, pond margins, night ice |
| overflow | overflow_bend | creek corridor | the same bend, at dusk, loaded |
| tracking/sign | tamarack ravens, willow runs | snares, ptarmigan | baited-run optimization |
| blaze navigation | blaze_gateway | the trapline both ways | the tunnel in near-whiteout |
| fire craft | squaw wood, birch bark | every camp | wind-engineering on the ice; ember transport |
| reading people | the wreck's story | Holt's sets, the yard | "leave the box full" |

The exams are the same rooms as the tutorials, revisited under worse conditions — the map
re-uses its geography instead of sprawling, which keeps travel knowledge compounding.

## 7. Story, as a walking order

The valley tells one story regardless of route order: *the crash is not the first thing
that ever happened here.* Ravens already commute to the wreck; the burn already regrew
fireweed; the beavers already solved winter; the survey cairn already measured the ridge;
Holt already blazed the way out and left the door open. Every room's story beat is a
variation on competence-before-you and the country's indifference-without-malice — which
is exactly the emotional argument for the players' own competence being possible. The
homestead lands it: the world is survivable because people keep leaving each other the
means.

# §8. Quality assessment — pass 1 (against good game design)

*The standard: not "is it big" but "is it enough, and is every piece load-bearing." Andrew's
bar: not dumbed down; findable-not-shoved; work for everything.*

**What holds up well:**
1. **Route-per-region with distinct scarce resources** directly implements GDD §37's
   hardest requirement and makes the map's SHAPE the rescue design. Strong spine.
2. **Pricing discipline** — the six-currency audit passes on every resource I re-checked;
   nothing usable is free-and-adjacent. The anti-easy rule survived drafting.
3. **Hazards are honest.** The telegraph audit (rooms.md tail) covers all 13; nothing
   kills unannounced; the one lethal zone is triple-warned and fully optional.
4. **Teaching structure** (tutorial → pay → exam in reused rooms) is real curriculum
   design and keeps the map compact-feeling despite 59 zones.

**Found issues (to fix in polish pass 1):**
- **I-1 (structural): the northeast is a dead end for the storm phase.** Ridge and strike
  path have no storm-phase role — once scouted, nothing recalls players NE, and the knob's
  radio window deserves a late-game reason to be braved in weather. *Fix direction: give
  the radio route a storm-phase beat (a heard voice mid-afternoon that makes a second
  climb tempting exactly when it's priced worst) — the risk/reward decision the NE lacks.*
- **I-2 (structural): water is over-solved in the south, under-solved in the north.**
  Riffle + water hole + pool all cluster south; the lake's blue ice is the only northern
  answer and it's fine — but the SIGNAL-camp party (lake-based) has no liquid option,
  which flattens their logistics day vs. the expedition party's. *Fix direction: the
  inlet's feeder brook should offer a dangerous-but-real northern draw — water inside the
  hazard zone, correctly priced, turning the inlet from pure tuition into a real trade.*
- **I-3 (economy): the sled arrives too late to matter for most sessions.** It's the best
  puzzle on the map and it unlocks at hour 3+ at the earliest, 2.4 km from everything it
  would move. Risk: most parties never experience the map's logistics endgame. *Fix
  direction: plant a CRUDE early sled option (a cowling/wing-panel drag — the plane's own
  skin as a toboggan, lashed with the existing paracord) so hauling gameplay starts at
  hour 1 in weak form and the woodshed sled is the UPGRADE, not the introduction.*
- **I-4 (fairness): the search-plane beat can silently no-op.** If the party is all-in on
  the cabin route, the plane passes an empty lake — correct consequence, but currently
  invisible (they'd never know). The gut-punch needs to be HEARD to teach. *Fix direction:
  the engine-drone is audible valley-wide (§15 AUDIBLE_ONLY band at minimum) with
  direction — every party learns the cost of their allocation, wherever they stand.*
- **I-5 (content gap): night is under-authored as terrain.** The report claims "three lit
  rooms" but no zone entry actually describes its night/storm variant; the presentation
  system supports state-conditioned looks and the design should hand it authored night
  reads for at least the handrail zones (tunnel, creek line, dooryard, ice_flat). *Fix:
  add night/storm look variants to the handrail zones' entries.*
- **I-6 (small): the birch stand's `game_trail_crossing` carries three jobs (antler, moose
  lesson, shortcut) while `aspen_fringe` carries half a job (poles + duplicate snare
  venue). Rebalance: fold the fringe's snare duplication into a pointer, give the fringe
  the stand's missing FOURTH job — the ember-carrier punk wood belongs there (dead-standing
  aspen IS the classic punk source), freeing the fallen birch to be purely about bark and
  making each of the four zones single-purpose-clear.*
- **I-7 (voice): three look entries drift toward cleverness-over-clarity ("administrative
  silence," "arithmetic," "like a decision through a committee") — flag for the prose
  pass; the style guide says write for the ear, and two of these survive reading aloud,
  one doesn't.*

**Verdict pass 1**: the skeleton is right (routes, economies, curriculum, story), the
issues are all fixable in place — two structural, two economy/fairness, two content, one
voice. Proceeding to polish pass 1 on the rooms.
