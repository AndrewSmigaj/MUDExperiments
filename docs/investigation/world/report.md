# How the rooms are used — the play report (v2, after polish pass 1)

> **Status: SCRATCHPAD (investigation) — 2026-07-15 overnight design run.** Companion to
> [`map.md`](map.md) and [`rooms.md`](rooms.md). This is the report Andrew asked for: how
> every room earns its place in an actual session. §8+ is the auditable process log — each
> assessment pass and what it changed. This v2 body reflects the rooms AFTER polish pass 1.

## 1. The session, told as rooms

A session is ~5 hours of daylight plus the storm night, on the real-time clock. The shape
of a competent-but-first-time party's day, told as movement through the map:

**Hour 1 — the wreck teaches.** The crash site plays as already built: dig through the
plane, split kit, frisk the pilot, learn the verbs. The first outdoor steps are short
radials: the treeline for tinder, the muskeg edge, the debris trail. The outer rooms are
all VISIBLE as invitations (lake-glare west from the fuselage top, the gouge pointing
northeast, running water faintly audible south on a still moment) but nothing forces the
door. The chart and the manual seed three plans at once — cabin, elevation, signals — and
the party can't fund all three. That argument is the game. Quietly load-bearing: the
engine cowling at the nose is a drag-toboggan for anyone who thinks of it, so the day's
logistics start crude and immediate rather than arriving gift-wrapped at hour four.

**Hour 2 — the first radial expeditions.** Whichever plan wins, its first leg crosses
teaching country: the muskeg's drifted channel punishes straight-line thinking on the way
to the lake; the strike path pays salvage immediately (boughs, the tire, the wing's drip)
on the way up; the north wood hands over squaw wood and the camp site on the way anywhere.
The tuition zones (drifted channel, inlet mouth, overflow bend) all sit on FIRST legs by
design — the map spends its cheap lessons early so the expensive country later is met with
skills, not luck. The inlet now teaches AND tempts in the same forty feet: the shore lead
is the lake party's water, priced in exactly the ice-reading the zone just taught.

**Hour 3 — commitment.** The stay-or-go fork stops being theoretical: the cabin round
trip (3 h first time) fits the remaining light exactly once, the search plane is coming
"sometime after midday" (the manual's SAR pages: overflights follow drainages — the lake
line), and the storm's first band is visibly filling the western sky from the knob, if
anyone climbed. Three coherent parties emerge naturally: **signal camp** (lake + gear
gouge + north-wood fuel ferries), **expedition** (creek → trapline → homestead), or
**split co-op** — the design's favorite: signalers hold the lake while runners make the
cabin.

**The beats land on everyone.** When the search plane comes, its drone is audible in
every room in the valley, with a bearing — the lake party gets its confidence event or
its scramble; the expedition party, a day's walk south, stops on the trail and HEARS the
cost of their allocation, which is the design working, not failing. Later, in the heavy
band, one garbled radio crackle reaches whoever carries the handheld: the knob's
second-climb invitation, priced at the day's worst rates, entirely declinable.

**Hour 4–5 — execution under weather.** Heavy snow re-prices everything (map.md §5): the
lake crossing closes, the spruce tunnel stays open, broken trails start refilling.
Whatever the party built by now — fire on the ice, wire snares working the runs, a sled
under repair in the woodshed — either pays or teaches.

**Night.** The world is three lit rooms: the stove, a fire, or the fuselage huddle. The
night phase plays IN whichever room the day's decisions bought, and its quality (warm
cabin with lamplight and beans, versus a smoky bough shelter, versus the shivering hull)
is the day's scorecard. The handrail zones now carry authored night/storm looks — the
tunnel that doesn't know about the storm, the bend read by steam alone, the yard of
shapes, the flat that erases your trail — so night play is authored terrain, not dimmed
day terrain. The tree-well hollow and the warmth-floor rule guarantee the worst night is
survivable if played straight: misery, not memory-wipe.

## 2. The four rescue routes, as room itineraries

GDD §37–39's additive-confidence model, each route's rooms and its DISTINCT scarce
resource (the §37 improvement made geographic):

| Route | Rooms | Scarce resource | Confidence events |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Stay-and-signal** | crash site, ice_flat, gear_gouge, north-wood fuel zones | *fuel logistics + wind engineering* | fire visible on ice; the TIRE's black smoke column; maintained through the plane beat |
| **Beacon (ELT §38)** | tail_section (the ELT), dooryard (cable) or avionics (wire), fuselage_top or the_knob | *conductor + elevation* | antenna rigged; elevation multiplier; the 121.5 manual clue closes the loop |
| **Radio (§38 deep puzzle)** | cockpit (the set), the wreck's batteries, the_knob | *carry logistics + weather windows* | static → voices on the knob; the heavy-band crackle; a working exchange in a clear pocket — the storm-phase second climb is this route's climax |
| **Travel/shelter** | creek run, trapline, homestead | *navigation skill + daylight* | the stove lit — survival secured buys the SECOND weather window; "we can outlast this" is rescue confidence too |

Every route crosses the survival economy (all fires eat the same wood, all trips the same
daylight) but no two routes compete for their KEY resource — wire, elevation, wind
engineering, and navigation are separate accounts.

## 3. The economies, as room networks

- **Fuel**: squaw wood (starter, north wood) → dwarf birch / krumm / black-spruce twigs
  (kindling, priced in armloads) → deadfall + logjam (bulk, tool-priced) → drift log +
  far burn + woodshed (jackpots, distance/tool-priced). Fire is always POSSIBLE; scale is
  always EARNED.
- **Water**: melt (anywhere + fuel tax) → blue ice (efficiency, lake) → the inlet's shore
  lead (north, nerve-priced) → riffle + water hole (south, travel-priced). Both halves of
  the map now run real water logistics.
- **Food**: rations (finite) → cranberries / rose hips (trickle) → grouse / ptarmigan
  (skill shots) → snare lines (planning + wire) → the fishery (the scaling source,
  longest chain) → cabin stores (the depot, farthest). Calories scale with commitment,
  never with luck.
- **Warmth/clothing**: crash clothing → the pilot's jacket → covers/blankets (DR-25) →
  marten fur (craft) → the loft trunk (jackpot); plus the terrain layer — exposure bands
  make WHERE you work a clothing decision.
- **Mobility & hauling**: bare boots → the cowling drag (hour-1 crude) → broken trails
  (self-made capital) → game trails / causeway / tunnel (the world's own roads,
  knowledge-priced) → snowshoes (cache) → the repaired freight sled (the endgame that
  makes the woodshed matter at the wreck). A full progression now, crude-to-mastery.
- **Fire-craft as its own arc**: lighter → squaw wood habit → birch bark (weatherproof
  start) → punk-cupped embers (portable flame, aspen fringe) → chaga spark (lighterless
  insurance) → avgas (dangerous shortcut). The birch stand's two zones split it cleanly:
  the grove starts fire anywhere, the fringe carries it forward.
- **Information**: the chart, the manual's pages, the pilot's line, the knob, the blaze
  protocol, ice-reading, track-reading. The only massless economy — which is why the
  knob, pure information, justifies the map's hardest climb.

## 5. Co-op, as geography

The map is sized so two-to-four players are RICHER than one, never merely faster: the
cache's shoulder-stand and the sled's ferry legs literally prefer two bodies, and the
signal/expedition split gives each half of a party a complete gameplay day that
RECOMBINES at night — the sled arriving at the lake fire with cabin wood is the co-op
fantasy image.

Communication across a split party is its own designed layer, and it is honest about
physics: voices carry zones, not kilometers (§15). Distant co-op runs on SIGHT — the
knob sees everything, the lake fire is visible valley-wide after dusk, smoke reads for
miles, and the manual's signals page teaches the old three-of-anything distress
convention (three smoke puffs, three shots, three flashes). A party that AGREES ON A
SMOKE CODE before splitting ("green boughs twice = come home") has invented long-range
messaging out of the fire system — the design's favorite emergent trick, fully supported
by existing systems (green boughs = white smoke is already authored; the tire = black).

## 6. How the world teaches (the knowledge ladder)

| Skill | Taught (cheap) | Pays | Examined (expensive) |
|---|---|---|---|
| snow travel | drifted_channel | everywhere | storm-phase travel |
| ice reading | inlet_mouth (3 telegraphs) | blue ice, crossings, the shore lead | confluence hole-chop, pond margins, night ice |
| overflow | overflow_bend | creek corridor | the same bend at dusk, loaded |
| tracking/sign | tamarack ravens, willow runs | snares, ptarmigan | baited-run optimization |
| blaze navigation | blaze_gateway | the trapline both ways | the tunnel in near-whiteout |
| fire craft | squaw wood, birch bark | every camp | wind-engineering on the ice; ember transport |
| reading people | the wreck's story | Holt's sets, the yard | "leave the box full" |

The exams are the tutorials' own rooms revisited under worse conditions — the map re-uses
its geography instead of sprawling, so travel knowledge compounds.

Behind every knowledge price stands an in-game teacher — mostly THE MANUAL, whose pages
(fire, water, shelter, signals, food, fishing, SAR, exposure) are now load-bearing
content; the pass-2 assessment below formalizes this.

## 7. Story, as a walking order

The valley tells one story regardless of route order: *the crash is not the first thing
that ever happened here.* Ravens already commute to the wreck; the burn already regrew
fireweed; the beavers already solved winter; the survey cairn already measured the ridge;
Holt already blazed the way out and left the door open. Every room's beat is a variation
on competence-before-you and indifference-without-malice — the emotional argument that
the players' own competence is possible. The homestead lands it: the world is survivable
because people keep leaving each other the means.

# §9. Assessment pass 2 — fresh angles (the questions pass 1 didn't ask)

*Pass 1 audited structure (routes, pricing, fairness). Pass 2 audits EXPERIENCE: who has
a good day, what failure feels like, whether an agent could play it, and what the design
silently demands from implementation.*

**A. Player-archetype coverage — does everyone get a full day?** Explorers: the whole
map, the knob, the bubble-trails — rich. Engineers: sled, windbreaks, antenna, ember
logistics — rich. Hunters/providers: snares, grouse, fishery — rich. Storytellers: Holt,
the letters, the note — rich. **Socializers/caretakers: thin.** The pilot is the only
care-target and he is scripted to fade; after him, care gameplay is warmth-nagging your
teammates. *Acceptable for scope (co-op itself is the social layer), but noted: the
drag-litter option (moving the pilot) is the one care-mechanic on the map — it should
stay.*

**B. Failure richness — is a bad day interesting?** The failure modes are graded misery
with exits, not spirals: wet boots → the emergency the manual rehearsed; lost in the
tunnel → backtrack protocol; missed plane → heard, mourned, teaches the second window;
night caught out → the bivvy. One EXCEPTION found: **a party that spends everything on
the cabin and arrives too exhausted/wet to work has no authored floor there** — the cabin
assumes competence on arrival (stove needs lighting, wood needs fetching). A soaked,
hypothermic arrival at a cold cabin is winnable in systems terms but reads as a wall.
*Fix for pass 3 (I-8): the cabin's kindling-already-laid detail (authored) is the floor —
make it explicit that ONE match's worth of competence lights it, and the woodshed's
door-side stack means the first armload is ten steps, not a chore chain. The homestead
should be the one place the world pre-paid the first move.*

**C. Agent-playability (the ontology goal) — can a text-reading agent solve this?**
Audit: every knowledge-priced resource was checked for an IN-GAME teacher. PASS with one
systemic finding: **the manual is now the world's textbook** — squaw wood, blue ice,
overflow probing, snare craft, signals, bulged cans, pool fishing, the bivvy all cite
it. That makes the manual's authored read-pages CRITICAL content equal in rank to the
zone looks. *Fix for pass 3 (I-9): rooms.md gains a cross-cutting section enumerating
the manual's required pages and which zone lessons each backs — the reading list becomes
a first-class deliverable.*

**D. The implementation bill (what the design silently demands).** New or extended
operations implied: `climb` (wing, chaga, cache, ladder), `probe` (drift, ice, overflow,
logjam — one op, many surfaces: arguably the map's signature verb), `throw` (grouse,
chaga), `drag/haul` (cowling, sled, litter), `set-snare` / `check-snare`, `fish`,
`shovel/dig` (exists), plus track persistence, exposure drain, and the timed-beat
scheduler (P4/P5/P7 seams already named). *Fix for pass 3 (I-10): a cross-cutting
"operations the map assumes" table in rooms.md, each mapped to its zones and its
graceful degradation (what the zone still offers if the op ships later) — so the world
can load ahead of its systems without dead zones.*

**E. Wonder pacing — dread/awe balance by region.** South and center are balanced
(bubble-trails, ptarmigan bursts, the dipper moon). The NORTH is dread-heavy: inlet,
cornice, the flat's erasure — awe up there is all scale and threat. *Fix for pass 3
(I-11): the north gets the aurora — a night-phase world event (a clear pocket tears
open mid-storm for minutes) with authored looks from the ice_flat, the knob, and the
dooryard. Free, load-bearing (aurora nights are the COLDEST — beauty priced in
temperature, true to life), and it gives night its one gift.*

**F. Replay and determinism.** The world is deterministic (locked); replay variety comes
from route economics, not shuffled loot — a second run differs because the PARTY differs
(knowledge carried forward re-prices everything; snowshoes-first is a different game).
This is the roguelike-with-fixed-seed model and it is fine for the experiment's goals;
the RNG decision stays parked in BACKLOG where it already lives. No fix needed — stance
documented here.

**G. The world remembers (second-visit prose).** Several entries promise memory (the
girdled birch, the drained dam, the cracked lodge, the pried shutter). That promise is an
appearance-registry bill: state-conditioned scene variants per remembered wound. *Fix for
pass 3 (I-12): a cross-cutting "memory states" list — every zone's rememberable changes
in one table, so the registry work is scoped and nothing promised in prose goes silent
in play.*

**Verdict pass 2**: the experience layer holds for all archetypes but caretakers
(accepted), failure is misery-with-exits except the cold-cabin arrival (fix), agents can
play it IF the manual ships complete (formalize), and the design's implementation bill
is real but seam-shaped (enumerate + degrade gracefully). Five fixes → pass 3.

# §10. Polish pass 3 — log (APPLIED) and the final verdict

All five pass-2 fixes landed in rooms.md: **I-8** the cabin's pre-paid first move (laid
kindling + door-side stack + the match tin — mercy in character, floor for the first
hour only) · **I-9** "The manual is the textbook" — the eight required pages, each mapped
to the zone lessons it backs, with the never-lie authoring rule · **I-10** "Operations
the map assumes" — ten ops/systems with zones and graceful degradation, so the world
loads ahead of its systems with no dead zones · **I-11** the aurora — night's one gift,
authored from the ice flat, the knob, and the dooryard, its beauty honestly priced in
plunging cold · **I-12** "The world remembers" — every prose-promised memory state in
one scoping table for the appearance registry. Plus a prose seam-clean: process
references stripped from zone entries (they live in this log), the grove's duplicated
phrasing merged.

**Final verdict.** Measured against Andrew's bar — *not dumbed down; is it ENOUGH?* —
the world now answers yes on every axis I know how to test: 59 zones across 11 Scenes,
four rescue routes on four separate scarce-resource accounts, six interlocking
economies each with a crude-to-mastery arc, a knowledge curriculum where every lesson
has a tutorial room, a paying room, and an exam, thirteen hazards telegraphed at least
twice each, every gate with at least two openings, every timed beat audible everywhere,
one deliberately lethal opt-in, one deliberate anti-loot lesson, one pre-paid mercy,
and a single story — competence-before-you — told by ravens, beavers, a surveyor, and
an absent man's laid kindling. Nothing on the map is free, and nothing on the map is
unfair; the remaining risks are authoring volume (the object pass in
[`objects.md`](objects.md) scopes it) and the implementation bill (§9-D, now enumerated
with degradations). The design is done arguing with itself; the next argument it needs
is Andrew's.
